Caesar Salad

Description

Caesar salad is a timeless dish known for its crisp romaine lettuce, crunchy croutons, and rich, creamy dressing. The dressing is typically made with garlic, anchovies, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, and Worcestershire sauce, blended with olive oil and mayonnaise for a smooth, savory flavor. Parmesan cheese adds a sharp, nutty note, balancing the dressing’s tanginess. Tossed together, these ingredients create a satisfying mix of textures and flavors: cool, crisp lettuce, cheesy and savory dressing, and crunchy croutons. Caesar salad is often enjoyed as a side but can also be topped with grilled chicken or shrimp for a heartier meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 large head of romaine lettuce, chopped
  • 1 cup croutons
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 2 anchovy fillets, minced (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste

Directions

  1. Wash and chop the romaine lettuce, then pat it dry and place it in a large bowl.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the minced garlic, anchovy fillets (if using), Dijon mustard, lemon juice, Worcestershire sauce, and mayonnaise until smooth.
  3. Slowly drizzle the olive oil into the dressing mixture while whisking, creating a creamy emulsion.
  4. Season the dressing with salt and black pepper to taste.
  5. Pour the dressing over the chopped romaine lettuce and toss until the leaves are evenly coated.
  6. Add croutons and sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese on top.
  7. Toss the salad gently to distribute the toppings evenly.
  8. Serve immediately, garnished with extra Parmesan cheese if desired.
