Korean Friend Chicken

Description

Korean fried chicken is a popular dish known for its ultra-crispy, crackling exterior and juicy, flavorful meat. Unlike traditional fried chicken, it's often double-fried, giving it an exceptionally crisp coating that stays crunchy, even when tossed in sauce. The chicken pieces are coated in a thin batter, fried once to cook the meat, and then fried again to add a light, shatteringly crisp crust. It’s commonly glazed with a sweet and spicy sauce made from ingredients like gochujang (Korean red chili paste), soy sauce, garlic, and sugar, balancing heat and sweetness. Korean fried chicken is typically garnished with sesame seeds and green onions and often served with pickled radish, making it an addictive snack or meal that’s beloved worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon onion powder
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • ½ teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 cup cold water
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • ¼ cup gochujang (Korean red chili paste)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey or sugar
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish
  • Sliced green onions, for garnish
  • Pickled radish, for serving

Directions

  1. Cut the chicken into small, manageable pieces, and set aside.
  2. In a bowl, mix the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, and baking powder.
  3. Slowly add the cold water to the dry ingredients, whisking until you have a smooth batter.
  4. Dip each piece of chicken into the batter, ensuring it's well-coated.
  5. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or fryer to 350°F (175°C).
  6. Fry the chicken pieces in batches to avoid overcrowding, cooking each for about 5–7 minutes, until golden.
  7. Remove the chicken, drain on paper towels, and let it rest for a few minutes.
  8. Double-fry the chicken by placing it back in the oil for another 3–4 minutes, making it extra crispy.
  9. In a separate bowl, combine gochujang, soy sauce, honey or sugar, rice vinegar, minced garlic, and sesame oil to make the sauce.
  10. Toss the double-fried chicken in the sauce until each piece is evenly coated.
  11. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.
  12. Serve hot with pickled radish on the side.
